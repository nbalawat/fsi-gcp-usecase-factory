The analyst is judging extraction faithfulness, the spread, peer context, collateral, related- party exposure, the rating, policy rules, the narrative, and finally sign-off. Inline-per-section beats sticky/modal because the analyst's eye never has to leave the data that informs the decision. Approve / edit / request- revision / reject sit AT THE END of every memo section — extraction, spread, peer, collateral, network, rating, rules, draft, final. The approval route mirrors the philosophy: it's a fast-track summary of inline decisions, not a separate review surface; jump-back links return the officer to the exact section that still needs attention.
